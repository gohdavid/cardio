## Refractory Periods

### Absolute Refractory Period (ARP)
- Period during which no stimulus â€” regardless of strength â€” can trigger a new action potential
- Corresponds to phases 0â€“2 of the ventricular action potential (inactivated Naâپ؛ channels)
- Prevents tetanic (sustained) contraction in the heart (unlike skeletal muscle)
- **The heart CANNOT tetanize** â€” this is an essential cardiac safety property

### Relative Refractory Period (RRP)
- Period during which a stronger-than-normal stimulus can trigger an action potential (phase 3)
- Action potentials generated in the RRP may be aberrant or trigger arrhythmias
- T-on-R phenomenon: PVC falling on the T wave (RRP) can trigger ventricular fibrillation

### Why the heart cannot tetanize
- ARP lasts nearly as long as mechanical systole
- The next action potential can only begin after mechanical relaxation has started
- This allows the ventricle to refill between beats

## Single Equivalent Moving Dipole: ECG Theory

### Concept
Each activated myocyte creates a local current dipole. The net sum of all active dipoles at any instant = **single equivalent moving dipole (m)**.

**Key insight**: Each ECG lead measures the **projection of m onto the lead axis**. Maximum signal when dipole aligns with lead vector; zero when perpendicular; negative when opposing.

### P Wave
- Originates at SA node (right atrium first, then left atrium)
- Net dipole vector: directed left, inferiorly, and anteriorly (toward lead II)
- Lead II shows the largest positive P wave (P axis normally +60آ°)

### QRS Complex
- Multiple vectors as septum, then anterior/inferior wall, then posterior/lateral LV depolarize
- Net QRS vector: mainly leftward and inferiorly directed (left axis from dominant LV mass)
- Begins with small rightward septal depolarization (Q wave in lateral leads)

### T Wave
- Ventricular repolarization
- T wave is upright (same direction as QRS) because epicardium repolarizes before endocardium
  - Epicardial APD is shorter than endocardial APD
  - Repolarization proceeds opposite direction to depolarization
  - Net effect: T wave vector aligns with QRS vector
